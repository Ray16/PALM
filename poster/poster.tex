% ── MOTIVATION ──
\begin{block}{Motivation}
Machine learning models in drug discovery, materials science, and bioinformatics are often evaluated with \textbf{randomly split} datasets. This practice introduces \textbf{data leakage}:

\vspace{0.8ex}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Structurally similar entities appear in \textbf{both} train and test sets
  \item Models memorize patterns rather than learning generalizable representations
  \item Reported metrics \textbf{overestimate} real-world performance
\end{itemize}

\vspace{1ex}
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[
    every node/.style={font=\large},
    box/.style={draw, rounded corners=4pt, minimum width=5cm, minimum height=1.8cm, align=center, line width=1.5pt},
]
  \node[box, fill=palmlight, draw=palmblue] (train) {Train Set\\{\small Drug A, Drug A$'$, Drug B}};
  \node[box, fill=red!8, draw=palmred, right=2cm of train] (test) {Test Set\\{\small Drug A$''$, Drug C}};
  \draw[-{Stealth[length=5mm]}, line width=2pt, palmred, dashed]
    (train.east) -- node[above, font=\Large\bfseries, palmred] {Leakage!} (test.west);
  \node[below=0.6cm of $(train)!0.5!(test)$, font=\large\color{palmgray}, align=center]
    {Drug A, A$'$, and A$''$ share $>$90\% structural similarity};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\vspace{1ex}
Our experiments show that random splitting inflates test R$^2$ by \textbf{0.23--0.33} on molecular property prediction (ESOL, Lipophilicity), revealing that apparent performance is largely driven by data leakage rather than genuine generalization.

\vspace{1ex}
\textbf{Goal:} Provide a unified, automated tool for \textbf{leakage-minimized splitting} across diverse scientific data domains.
\end{block}

% ── BACKGROUND ──
\begin{block}{Background}

\textbf{Splitting Hierarchy} --- from least to most stringent:

\vspace{0.5ex}
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[
    level/.style={font=\large, rounded corners=3pt, draw, minimum width=7cm, minimum height=1.5cm, align=center, line width=1.2pt},
]
  \node[level, fill=red!8, draw=palmred] (R) at (0,0) {\textbf{R} --- Random split (baseline)};
  \node[level, fill=orange!8, draw=palmorange] (I1) at (0,-2.2) {\textbf{I1} --- No identity overlap on one axis};
  \node[level, fill=yellow!8, draw=palmorange!80!black] (I2) at (0,-4.4) {\textbf{I2} --- No identity overlap on either axis};
  \node[level, fill=green!6, draw=palmgreen!80!black] (C1) at (0,-6.6) {\textbf{C1} --- Cluster-based split (one axis)};
  \node[level, fill=green!12, draw=palmgreen] (C2) at (0,-8.8) {\textbf{C2} --- Cluster-based (both axes, strictest)};
  \draw[-{Stealth[length=4mm]}, line width=2pt, palmgray] (R.south) -- (I1.north);
  \draw[-{Stealth[length=4mm]}, line width=2pt, palmgray] (I1.south) -- (I2.north);
  \draw[-{Stealth[length=4mm]}, line width=2pt, palmgray] (I2.south) -- (C1.north);
  \draw[-{Stealth[length=4mm]}, line width=2pt, palmgray] (C1.south) -- (C2.north);
  \node[right=0.8cm of C2, font=\large\color{palmgreen}, align=left] {$\leftarrow$ Increasing\\rigor};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\vspace{1ex}
\textbf{DataSAIL} (Data Splitting Against Information Leakage) formulates splitting as a constrained optimization problem, minimizing cross-split similarity while respecting ratio constraints.

\vspace{0.8ex}
\textbf{PALM} extends DataSAIL with:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Domain-specific \textbf{feature extraction} for 4 entity types
  \item \textbf{Adaptive distance metrics} (Euclidean, cosine, Tanimoto) selected based on feature sparsity
  \item \textbf{17+ input format} support with automatic detection
  \item \textbf{Quality metrics} and \textbf{visualizations}
  \item An interactive \textbf{web application}
\end{itemize}
\end{block}

\begin{block}{Discussion}

\textbf{Key Contributions:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Unified framework} for leakage-aware splitting across four scientific domains (molecules, biomolecules, materials, genes) with a single configuration interface
  \item \textbf{Adaptive algorithm selection:} automatic distance metric and dimensionality reduction based on feature sparsity --- Tanimoto for binary fingerprints, cosine for sparse embeddings, PCA for very high-dimensional data
  \item \textbf{Format preservation:} input and output in the same scientific format (SDF, FASTA, CIF, etc.), enabling direct integration into existing ML workflows
  \item \textbf{Quantitative evaluation:} built-in metrics (NN leakage, distribution shift, coverage) provide objective evidence of split quality
\end{itemize}

\vspace{1ex}
\textbf{Limitations \& Future Work:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item C2 splitting on very sparse, high-dimensional datasets may yield low coverage --- adaptive constraint relaxation partially mitigates this
  \item Scaling to datasets with $>$100K entities requires further optimization of distance computation
  \item Future directions include temporal splits for time-series data, active learning integration, and pre-computed distance matrix support for custom similarity functions
\end{itemize}

\vspace{1ex}
\textbf{Impact:}
By making rigorous splitting accessible through both CLI and web interfaces, PALM lowers the barrier for researchers to evaluate their models under realistic conditions --- helping the community move toward \textbf{more honest benchmarking} in scientific ML.
\end{block}
